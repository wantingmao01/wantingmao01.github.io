About Me

I'm a second-year PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, advised by Prof. Jim Rehg. Before my PhD, I completed dual B.S. degrees in Data Science and Applied Mathematics at UC San Diego.

My research focuses on AI for healthcare with a current emphasis on self-supervised learning for time-series Foundation Models. My goal is to translate foundation models for biosignals into safe, equitable, real-world impact. Borrowing Bayer's slogan, my motto is: Science for a Better Life.

ProtoMM: Leveraging Shared Prototypes for a Multimodal Pulse Motion Foundation Model

ProtoMM introduces a prototype-based framework for multimodal self-supervised learning on time-series data. By clustering embeddings around shared prototypes, ProtoMM discovers common physiological states and achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse tasks.
